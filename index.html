I am Ansgarius, a passionate political scientist currently pursuing my PhD. My research is dedicated to exploring critical issues within the realms of supplemental poverty standards, indigenous politics, and the unique challenges faced by archipelagic and small island nations. My work is focused on understanding how political structures, economic policies, and governance systems impact vulnerable populations, particularly those often overlooked in mainstream political discourse.

My journey in political science is driven by a deep commitment to uncovering the political and economic factors that shape the lives of marginalized communities, especially within small, often isolated, island nations. Through my research, I aim to provide a fresh lens on how we can better address poverty and political inequality in these distinctive contexts, with a focus on equity, sustainability, and cultural preservation.

Research Focus

  • Redefining Poverty Metrics for Marginalized Communities
  • Indigenous Governance and Political Autonomy
  • Political Resilience in Small Island Nations
